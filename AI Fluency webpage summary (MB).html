The AI Fluency Framework

Four interconnected competencies necessary to ensure our interactions with AI are effective, efficient, ethical, and safe.

The 4D Framework

Delegation

Using your expertise and judgement to decide whether, when, and how to engage with AI. The goal isn't to automate everything, but to enable an effective partnership that leverages the unique strengths of you and the AI.

• Your ability to define your own goals and understand what work is needed before involving AI tools.
• A working knowledge of AI systems and their capabilities and limitations.
• The strategic process of breaking down complex tasks and dividing work between humans and AI.
▼

Description

Communicating with AI in ways that create a productive and collaborative environment. Clear communication upfront saves time and leads to better results.

• The ability to craft clear, specific, prompts, and instructions to define your desired output.
• Providing appropriate guidance, context, and constraints.
• Iterating on prompts to improve results.
• Understanding how different AI systems respond to various input styles.
▼

Discernment

Your ability to thoughtfully and critically evaluate what AI produces, how it produces it, and how it behaves. This requires your expertise and an understanding of AI systems and their typical limitations.

• Evaluating AI output accuracy, quality, and relevance.
• Identifying potential biases or limitations.
• Recognizing when AI responses may be incomplete or incorrect.
• Validating AI-generated content against reliable sources.
▼

Diligence

Taking responsibility for what we do and create with AI, and how we do it. AI systems and our interactions with them exist in context of many other obligations, systems, and people.

• Ensuring ethical use of AI tools and outputs.
• Understanding privacy and security implications.
• Maintaining trust by taking responsibility and being transparent about AI-assisted or generated outputs you share with others.
• Continuously learning about AI capabilities and limitations.
▼

The '4D' competencies apply across all our interactions with AI systems

There are three main ways we interact with AI systems.

Automation

AI executes specific tasks based on pre-defined instructions

Augmentation

Humans and AI collaborate as thinking partners to tackle a project, problem, or goal

Agency

Humans configure AI to independently perform future tasks on their behalf
